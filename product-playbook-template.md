# Product Playbook Template

This template outlines how I structure product playbooks to prepare teams for go-to-market readiness.

---

## 1. Executive summary

- Quick summary of the document - "WHY WHAT HOW and WHO" section

---

## 2. Introduction

- Product official name
- Where the product sits in the product portfolio highlighting if its an anchor product, standalone product, or an add-on product
- Org structure and roles contacts for the product across product, sales, customer success, marketing and product marketing teams

---

## 3. Market context

- Different market segments and catergories
- Market category
- Key trends
- Strategic opportunity/market outlook
- Core story of the product -> why does it exist
  
--- 

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Have three sections of Ideal fit, good fit and disqualifiers showing below details
- Target industries
- Company size
- Technographics
- Business context
- Org set-up and resources
- Buying centres

---

## 5. Deep dives into the buying centres

Atleast a page for each personas clearly markting their role in the buying centre - Decision maker, gatekeeper, user, influenecer, economic buyer/sponsor. Have these sections for each persona/buying centre
- Role
- Place in org structure
- Pains
- Goals
- KPIs
- Channels to reach them

---

## 6. Competitive landscape

Primary competitors with details on who are they, what they do well, what they DONT do well, how can we win/position ourselves. Inlcuding details on offerings, pricing and packaging etc.
- competitor A
- competitor B
- competitor C

---

## 7. Product details

- Introduction to production - what it is, what is does
- Product Process structure (if available) - inputs -> outputs
- Product architecture 
- Capability (main capabilities of the product) and feature (Key features under each capability) framework
- Key use cases of product
- Key benefits of product e,g. efficiency increase by x%, uptime % etc.
- USPs, key differentiators of product - Through market and competition what problems and solutions do we offer UNIQUELY + Supporting proof points

---

## 8. Customer landscape/references/logos (in-case of existing products. The same can be given for adjacent product in case its a new product)

- Customer footprint segemented by geographies, industries, customer size and/or other slicing that you have. This is particularly helpful to find references easily during an on-going deal. This can be directly fed to the CRM tool as well
- Detailed customer stories/testimonials
- Details on where and how you can use each testimonial/story

---

## 9. Key product narratives

- Core value proposition
- Messaging theme and supporting pillars -> this is extremely helpful to create an integrated marketing for the product across channels

---

## 10. FAQs & objections

Common questions:
- Question 1 -> e.g. When is the next release -> Answer for question.
- Question 2 -> e.g. Where can we find more details about this use case ->  Answer for question.
   
Common objections from prospects:
- objection 1 -> how to answer/handle it with facts and figures
- objection 2 -> how to answer/handle it with facts and figures
  
---

## 11. Product roadmap and other key milestones

- Roadmap items -> what this means for customers (value story)
- Timelines
- Key contacts

## 12. Pricing and packaging

- Pricing structure
- Packages
- Any seasonal campaigns
- Key links -> where to find pricing, approvers, process etc

## 13. Launch readiness checklist and timelines, in case of new products (Gantt chart is most understandable)

- Messaging finalized
- ICP defined
- Competitive positioning documented
- Internal enablement completed
- Sales team training (link to recording if already done)

---

## 14. Internal Enablement

- Who needs this document?
  - product marketing
  - sales
  - customer success
  - leadership
- Other resources with links
  - Webisites
  - Sales decks
  - Trainings
  - Other tools
  - Market activation assets like whitepapers, blogs etc
